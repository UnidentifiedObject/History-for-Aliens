Roman Empire 27 BC - 476 AD

Rome ruled the Mediterranean and much of Europe, Western Asia and North Africa. It is known for its advancements in engineering, law, and military strategy and "Pax romana".
"Pax Romana" was the golden age for roman empire. Starts with Augustus and ends in 180AD with the death of Marcus Aurelius.

Landmarks:

The Colosseum

Located in Rome, this ancient amphitheater is one of the most famous landmarks of the Roman Empire and was used for gladiatorial contests.

Ephesus

Ephesus was once a major trade and cultural center in the Roman Empire, home to the grand Temple of Artemis, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
