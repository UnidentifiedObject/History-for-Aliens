French Revolution

The French Revolution, which began in 1789, was a period of political and social upheaval in France, marked by the overthrow of the monarchy and the rise of radical political ideologies. It led to significant changes, including the establishment of the First French Republic and the spread of revolutionary ideas across Europe.
