The Renaissance

The Renaissance was a cultural, artistic, and intellectual movement that began in Italy during the 14th century and spread across Europe, marking the transition from the Middle Ages to the Modern era. It emphasized a revival of classical learning, humanism, and the development of new artistic techniques, leading to groundbreaking achievements in art, science, and philosophy.

Landmarks:

The Duomo of Florence

This iconic cathedral dome is a remarkable feat of engineering and architecture, representing the innovation of the Renaissance period.

The Louvre Museum in Paris

Originally a royal palace, it became one of the most famous art museums in the world, housing Renaissance masterpieces.
