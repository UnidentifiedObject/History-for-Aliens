Industrial Revolution

The Industrial Revolution was a period of rapid industrialization and technological innovation that began in the late 18th century in Britain, transforming economies, societies, and manufacturing processes. It introduced key inventions like the steam engine and mechanized textiles, leading to urbanization.

Events:

The Development of the Steam Engine by James Watt in 1776

James Watt's improvements to the steam engine were crucial in powering factories, trains, and ships.

The Invention of the Telegraph in 1837

Samuel Morse's creation of the telegraph revolutionized communication, allowing for long-distance messaging and greatly impacting business and industry.
